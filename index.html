Funnl

Funnl is an ongoing web app, first developed at HackMIT. We believed that reading news is important, but too often are we stuck re-reading background information that all of our favorite news site all share. We want the unique content, but don't want to click through ten different news outlets to get it. So we developed Funnl, a webapp that aggregates the unique content across news sources and diplays it in an intuitive way for an efficient reading experience. Written in Flask, but currently in progress to switch to a Node.js app for better scalability.

#Hashtag Gloves

Features on national news sites, #Hashtag Gloves was a hackathon project that went viral. We developed Twitter-integrated gloves that would use voice recognition to 'tweet' whatever you said when you tapped your fingers together twice in the 'hashtag' sign. Inspired by a Jimmy Fallon sketch, we won 3rd place at HackHolyoke in the Fall of 2014. Used Twitter and Google Voice APIs

Uber Instant

Uber Instant was a project for HackHolyoke Spring 2015. We developed a physical Uber interface that used an Arduino, LCD screen, and buttons to connect to the Uber API. When the buttons were pressed, we used voice recognition software to detect the destination the user wanted to get to, and then called an Uber to their current location. The LCD screen displayed the information about the ride, including destination, time until the Uber arrived, and the cost of the ride. We won 2nd place at the Hackathon!

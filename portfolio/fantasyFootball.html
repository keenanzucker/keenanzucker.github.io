Fantasy Football Algorithms

Fall 2015

For my Linearity II project, I worked with another student on how to optimize a Fantasy Football draft. As we were both fantasy football fans, we wanted to see if we could write a program that would give us the team with the highest chance of success. This becomes a tricky problem because where is it relatively simple to evaluate two players at the same position (one running back is better than another), it is significantly more difficult to evaluate players at different positions.

We first looked at this problem through the lens of a large game tree. Each pick, there would be several options for the team owner to choose from. As this tree builds out, it becomes incredibly large, but if we could evaluate it, then we in theory could find the 'optimal' solution.So we looked into different methods of pruning the tree. We learned about game theory, minimaxing, alpha-beta pruning, and other methods. Unfortunately, even with these methods implemented, our tree was too large to be evaluated on even a supercomputer.

We then pivoted to develop several 'picking' algorithms, and then test them against one another to develop a strategy that would be 'pretty good,' but would not be the objective solution like we had hoped. We wrote a Python program to do this and gave a ten minute presentation on our findings.
